# Human induced pluripotent stem cell-derived cardiomyocytes as an electrophysiological model: Opportunities and challenges—The Hamburg perspective

**Authors:** Djemail Ismaili, Carl Schulz, Thomas Eschenhagen, Torsten Christ, Arne Hansen, Donatus Mika, Jussi T. Koivumäki, András Horváth  
**Year:** 2023  
**Venue:** Frontiers in Physiology 14:1132165  
**File:** [[raw/schulz-2023-hamburg.pdf]]  
**DOI:** 10.3389/fphys.2023.1132165

> **Citation note:** main.md lists first author as "Schulz, C." with "Ismaili, D." last. The actual PDF has Ismaili as first author; Schulz is second. This is a citation ordering error in the review draft.

## Contribution
A Hamburg-group review of the opportunities and limitations of hiPSC-CM electrophysiological models, covering ion channel expression (If, ICa,T), action potential recording challenges, atrial cell models, and automated patch-clamp platforms.

## Methodology
Narrative review drawing on the group's experimental experience in Hamburg. Covers: differentiation maturity, ion channel expression differences from adult CMs, recording conditions (patch clamp vs. MEA), atrial-like vs. ventricular-like phenotypes, and automated high-throughput electrophysiology.

## Findings
- hiPSC-CMs express funny current (If) and T-type calcium current (ICa,T), which are absent or minimal in mature adult ventricular cardiomyocytes, creating a neonatal-like phenotype.
- Action potential recordings reveal significant heterogeneity: ventricular-, atrial-, and nodal-like subtypes coexist in typical preparations.
- MEA field potential duration (FPD) serves as a surrogate for QT interval but its physiological interpretation is complicated by immature ion channel expression.
- Automated patch clamp platforms provide scalable AP recordings but require careful selection of cell subtypes and recording conditions.
- Rate correction of FPD (FPDc) using clinical formulas (Fridericia) is the common approach despite not being validated for hiPSC-CM beating rates.

## Limitations
Narrative review; does not present new primary data. Perspectives reflect a single group's experience and may not generalize to all hiPSC-CM lines or platforms.

## Relevance to our paper
Cited in main.md as a source for the broad challenges and opportunities of hiPSC-CM electrophysiological models (intro section). Provides conceptual context for why FPD analysis conventions matter and why maturity-related ion channel differences limit direct translation to clinical QT interpretation.
